UI Design

Basically, deciding how an application is going to loook like. This requires a vast knowledge of color schemes, typography, looks and feels, dimensions, graphic design and a high sense of aesthetics. This is actually one of my favorite steps on the development process.

Prototyping

These are simluations or sample versions of a final product that are used for testing before launch. The goal of a prototype is to validate ideas before sharing them with stakeholders o engineers. Also, it saves you money by identifying and fixing errors before development.

Wireframing

Wireframes are used to set the structure of the product, it focuses more on functionality rather than on looks and visuals. These are essential on any product development

User Research

In order to create an optimal product, you need UX research. Gathering data to back the design processs and the decisions made. This will also help you identify the users who will use your product

Information Architecture

A well-designed, user-friendly information architecture ensures that users spend less time and effort searching for information and are successful in finding what they need.

User testing

There are many reasons of why User testing is important. Increasing your revenue, conversion rates, customer retention, reducing costs, fast improvement, quick adaptability, and many more. Knowing the methods and how to apply them in the best way will also help, and there is where I come in!

Web Design

From wireframing, UI and prototyping to having it laid out with code (HTML + CSS + Javascript). And also hosted on the internet for preview. This is one of my strongest skills being a front-end developer.

Mobile App Design

Being Android or iOS, I'm able to perform a complete mobile app design. This is critical if you take into consideration that user experience is now one of the most common reasons of why people choose to use an app, even though there could be many other similar apps.

3D Modeling

This is really a plus for any designer. A 3D modeling design can really stand out in any web page. It not only looks great, but can add a significant visual value to your brand
